Random in Tandem

There are many other existing "sanity checks" and evaluation metrics for saliency maps. Unfortunately, many of these rely on some human intuition about what a saliency map should look like. The "model randomness test" proposed by Adebayo et al. (2018) whether or not randomizing a model's weights changes the saliency map produces by the model, with the idea that if it doesn't, the saliency map is independent of the model itself. But this makes the assumption that a trained model's "true" saliency map doesn't look like a random model's saliency map. We don't have any a priori reason to believe that either!
