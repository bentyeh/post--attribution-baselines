Putting on the Training Wheels

We assume an underlying gaussian or uniform distribution because it is easy to draw samples from them. Although we do not have access to our data's underlying distribution, we do have many samples from that distribution: the training data! We can use those samples to define our baseline! We can again break down expected gradients with the training distribution in three parts: $$ \frac{1}{k} \sum_{j=1}^k \underbrace{(x_i - x'^j_i) \times \frac{\delta f(\text{ } \overbrace{x'^j + \alpha^{j} (x - x'^j)}^{\text{(1): Interpolated Image}} \text{ })}{\delta x_i}}_{\text{ (2): Gradients at Interpolation}} = \overbrace{\hat{\phi_i}^{EG}(f, x, k; D_{\text{data}})} ^{\text{(3): Cumulative Gradients up to }\alpha} $$
