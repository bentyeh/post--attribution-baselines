Being (de)coy with MNIST

Certain baselines may produce results that look better than others, but is a flawed way to judge saliency methods. We don't actually know what the model has learned: we are simply assuming that the model has learned the input output relationship we find most intuitive and obvious. There is no a priori reason to assume the model has learned that relationship! In extreme cases, data poisoning or pathological confounders can cause very strange looking saliency maps.
